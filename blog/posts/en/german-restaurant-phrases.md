# German Restaurant Phrases: Order Food Like a Local (Even with Zero German)

You're hungry in Germany. Found a cute restaurant. Sat down at a table.

Then the waiter approaches, speaking German rapidly, and suddenly...

**FREEZE.** What do you say? How do you order? How do you ask for the bill without looking like a confused tourist?

Welcome to your complete guide to **German restaurant phrases and dining vocabulary** — the survival kit for anyone eating out in German-speaking countries.

## Why German Dining Culture Is Different

**German restaurants have unique expectations:**

- **Table reservations:** Often required, especially for dinner
- **Cash payments:** Many places still prefer cash
- **Tipping culture:** Rounding up, not percentage-based
- **Water isn't free:** You'll pay for bottled water
- **Bread baskets:** Sometimes complimentary, sometimes charged

**The reality:** Germans appreciate when visitors make an effort with German, but most restaurant staff speak some English.

## Essential Arrival Phrases

**Start every dining experience right:**

### Entering the Restaurant

1. **Hallo, ist hier noch frei?**
   (Hello, is this seat still free?)
   - **Use:** Checking for available tables
   - **Context:** Casual restaurants, cafes

2. **Ich habe eine Reservierung auf [Name]**
   (I have a reservation under [Name])
   - **Use:** Arriving with booking
   - **Context:** Reserved tables

3. **Für wie viele Personen?**
   (For how many people?)
   - **Use:** Host asking about party size
   - **Response:** "Für zwei/drei/vier" (For two/three/four)

### Getting Seated

4. **Hier entlang, bitte**
   (This way, please)
   - **Use:** Host guiding you to table
   - **Context:** Follow them

5. **Hier ist die Speisekarte**
   (Here is the menu)
   - **Use:** Receiving menu
   - **Response:** "Danke schön" (Thank you very much)

## Reading the German Menu

**Menu survival vocabulary:**

### Menu Sections

6. **Vorspeise**
   (Appetizers)
   - **Use:** First course
   - **Examples:** Suppen (soups), Salate (salads)

7. **Hauptgericht**
   (Main course)
   - **Use:** Main dishes
   - **Examples:** Fleisch (meat), Fisch (fish), Vegetarisch (vegetarian)

8. **Beilagen**
   (Side dishes)
   - **Use:** Accompaniments
   - **Examples:** Kartoffeln (potatoes), Gemüse (vegetables)

9. **Nachspeise**
   (Desserts)
   - **Use:** Sweet endings
   - **Examples:** Kuchen (cake), Eis (ice cream)

10. **Getränke**
    (Drinks)
    - **Use:** Beverages
    - **Examples:** Wasser (water), Bier (beer), Wein (wine)

### Dietary Restrictions

11. **Ich bin Vegetarier/Vegan**
    (I am vegetarian/vegan)
    - **Use:** Dietary requirements
    - **Context:** Important for menu navigation

12. **Ich habe eine Allergie gegen [Nuss/Milch]**
    (I have an allergy against [nuts/milk])
    - **Use:** Food allergies
    - **Context:** Safety first

13. **Ist das glutenfrei?**
    (Is that gluten-free?)
    - **Use:** Celiac requirements
    - **Context:** Increasingly common in Germany

## Ordering Food and Drinks

**Make your selections like a pro:**

### Getting the Waiter's Attention

14. **Entschuldigung?**
    (Excuse me?)
    - **Use:** Getting waiter's attention politely
    - **Context:** Raise hand slightly, make eye contact

15. **Könnten ich bitte bestellen?**
    (Could I order please?)
    - **Use:** Ready to order
    - **Context:** Polite, direct

### Ordering Your Meal

16. **Ich hätte gerne [dish]**
    (I would like [dish])
    - **Use:** Polite ordering
    - **Example:** "Ich hätte gerne das Schnitzel" (I would like the schnitzel)

17. **Für mich [dish]**
    (For me [dish])
    - **Use:** Casual ordering
    - **Context:** More direct, still polite

18. **Nehmen Sie auch [dish]?**
    (Do you also take [dish]?)
    - **Use:** Asking about availability
    - **Context:** Item not listed or sold out

### Ordering Drinks

19. **Ein Wasser, bitte**
    (A water, please)
    - **Use:** Still water
    - **Context:** Usually bottled, not free

20. **Ein Wasser ohne Kohlensäure**
    (A water without carbonation)
    - **Use:** Still water (non-sparkling)
    - **Context:** Important distinction in Germany

21. **Ein Bier, bitte**
    (A beer, please)
    - **Use:** Ordering beer
    - **Context:** Specify size if asked (klein/groß)

22. **Ein Glas Wein**
    (A glass of wine)
    - **Use:** Wine by glass
    - **Context:** Rot (red) or Weiß (white)

## During the Meal

**Navigate dining like a local:**

### Compliments and Complaints

23. **Das schmeckt ausgezeichnet**
    (That tastes excellent)
    - **Use:** Paying compliment to chef/staff
    - **Context:** Always appreciated

24. **Das ist lecker**
    (That is delicious)
    - **Use:** Casual compliment
    - **Context:** Show enjoyment

25. **Das ist zu kalt**
    (That is too cold)
    - **Use:** Food temperature issue
    - **Context:** Polite complaint

26. **Entschuldigung, das ist nicht, was ich bestellt habe**
    (Excuse me, that is not what I ordered)
    - **Use:** Wrong order
    - **Context:** Mistakes happen

### Asking for Extras

27. **Könnte ich noch etwas Brot bekommen?**
    (Could I get some more bread?)
    - **Use:** Requesting more bread
    - **Context:** Sometimes free, sometimes charged

28. **Noch etwas Wasser?**
    (Some more water?)
    - **Use:** Refill request
    - **Context:** Simple, direct

## Getting the Bill

**German payment etiquette:**

### Asking for the Bill

29. **Die Rechnung, bitte**
    (The bill, please)
    - **Use:** Standard request
    - **Context:** Universal German phrase

30. **Zahlen, bitte**
    (To pay, please)
    - **Use:** Ready to pay
    - **Context:** Signal to bring bill

31. **Getrennt oder zusammen?**
    (Separate or together?)
    - **Use:** Splitting the bill
    - **Context:** "Getrennt" (separate checks) or "zusammen" (one bill)

### Paying

32. **Bar oder Karte?**
    (Cash or card?)
    - **Use:** Payment method
    - **Context:** "Bar" (cash) or "Karte" (card)

33. **Stimmt so**
    (That's right / That's correct)
    - **Use:** Confirming bill amount
    - **Context:** Checking total

34. **Keep the change**
    (Understanding: "Stimmt so" means you don't want change back)
    - **Use:** Including tip in payment
    - **Context:** Rounding up is common German tipping

## German Tipping Culture

**How to tip in Germany:**

### Tipping Etiquette

35. **Das stimmt so**
    (That's right — keep the change)
    - **Use:** Rounding up as tip
    - **Amount:** Usually 5-10% rounding

36. **Danke, den Rest können Sie behalten**
    (Thanks, you can keep the rest)
    - **Use:** Explicit tipping
    - **Context:** More generous tipping

### German Tipping Rules

- **Restaurants:** Round up 5-10% (not 15-20% like US)
- **Cafes:** Round to nearest euro
- **Bars:** Round up or small tip per drink
- **Service included:** "Bedienung ist im Preis enthalten" means tip not required

## Special Dining Situations

**Handle unique German dining scenarios:**

### Biergarten (Beer Garden)

37. **Ist hier noch frei?**
    (Is this seat still free?)
    - **Use:** Finding shared table
    - **Context:** Beer gardens often have communal seating

38. **Ein Mass, bitte**
    (A liter of beer, please)
    - **Use:** Ordering large beer
    - **Context:** "Mass" = one liter

### Bakery (Bäckerei)

39. **Ich hätte gerne ein Brötchen**
    (I would like a bread roll)
    - **Use:** Ordering bread
    - **Context:** Point to what you want

40. **Das da, bitte**
    (That one there, please)
    - **Use:** Pointing at display items
    - **Context:** Simple, effective

### Cafe (Konditorei)

41. **Ein Kaffee und ein Stück Kuchen**
    (A coffee and a piece of cake)
    - **Use:** Classic cafe order
    - **Context:** Afternoon treat

42. **Haben Sie Kuchen mit [Obst/Schokolade]?**
    (Do you have cake with [fruit/chocolate]?)
    - **Use:** Cake preference
    - **Context:** Common flavors: Apfel (apple), Schokolade (chocolate)

## Cultural Dining Tips

**Navigate German dining like a local:**

### DO ✅

- **Make reservations:** Especially for dinner on weekends
- **Greet when entering:** "Hallo" or "Guten Abend"
- **Say please and thank you:** "Bitte" and "Danke" go far
- **Wait to be seated:** Don't just grab any table
- **Round up bills:** German tipping culture

### DON'T ❌

- **Expect free water:** Water costs money in Germany
- **Tip 15-20%:** 5-10% rounding is standard
- **Snap fingers at waiters:** Rude everywhere
- **Leave without paying:** Obvious but worth stating
- **Assume English is spoken:** Learn basic phrases first

## Regional German Specialties

**Try local dishes where available:**

- **Bavaria:** Weißwurst (white sausage), pretzels
- **North:** Labskaus (fish roll), Herring
- **Rhine:** Sauerbraten (marinated meat)
- **South:** Maultaschen, Spätzle (egg noodles)

## The Bottom Line

German restaurant phrases don't need to be perfect — they just need to be functional and polite.

Restaurant staff will appreciate:
- **Basic politeness:** Please and thank you in German
- **Clear communication:** Pointing works if words fail
- **Patience:** German service can be slower than you're used to
- **Cultural respect:** Follow German dining customs

**Pro tip:** Start with "Die Speisekarte, bitte" (The menu, please) and "Ich hätte gerne..." (I would like...) — these two will get you through 90% of restaurant situations. And remember: *Wasser ohne Kohlensäure* means still water, not sparkling — a crucial distinction in German dining!
