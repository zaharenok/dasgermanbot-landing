# German Idioms & Sayings: 30 Expressions That Make No Sense in English

You're learning German, feeling confident, and then you hear:

*Ich verstehe nur Bahnhof.* (I only understand train station.)

*Tomaten auf den Augen haben.* (To have tomatoes on one's eyes.)

*Da steppt der Bär.* (That's where the bear dances.)

Wait... what?

Welcome to the wonderful world of German idioms (Redewendungen). They're colorful, confusing, and absolutely essential for sounding like a native speaker.

## Why Learn German Idioms?

**Textbook German = Boring. German idioms = REAL.**

Germans LOVE their idiomatic expressions. They use them daily in:
- Casual conversations
- Business meetings
- News and media
- Literature and films

**The reality:** If you only learn textbook German, you'll miss half of what people actually mean.

## The Top 30 German Idioms You Need to Know

**Master these 30 and you'll sound like you've lived in Germany for years:**

### Confusion & Understanding

1. **Ich verstehe nur Bahnhof** (I only understand train station)
   - Meaning: I don't understand anything at all
   - *Er hat so schnell geredet, ich verstehe nur Bahnhof.* (He spoke so fast, I didn't understand anything.)

2. **Schwiegal im Nebel** (Swimming in fog)
   - Meaning: Being confused or lost
   - *Ohne Anleitung bin ich völlig schwall im Nebel.* (Without instructions, I'm completely lost.)

3. **Nicht auf den Kopf gefallen sein** (Not to have fallen on one's head)
   - Meaning: To be smart/clever
   - *Du bist ja nicht auf den Kopf gefallen.* (You're pretty smart, aren't you?)

### Problems & Challenges

4. **Das ist nicht mein Bier** (That's not my beer)
   - Meaning: That's not my business/problem
   - *Warum er sich nicht meldet? Das ist nicht mein Bier.* (Why he's not responding? That's not my problem.)

5. **Die Sache verkomplizieren** (To complicate the matter)
   - Meaning: To make things more complex
   - *Verkomplizier das Problem nicht.* (Don't complicate the problem.)

6. **Sich den Kopf zerbrechen** (To break one's head)
   - Meaning: To think hard about something
   - *Ich zerbreche mir den Kopf, aber ich finde die Lösung nicht.* (I'm thinking hard but can't find the solution.)

### Emotions & Reactions

7. **Da bin ich aus dem Häuschen** (I'm out of the little house)
   - Meaning: I'm delighted/excited
   - *Als ich die Nachricht hörte, war ich aus dem Häuschen.* (When I heard the news, I was delighted.)

8. **Sich über etwas ärgern** (To get angry about something)
   - Meaning: To be annoyed
   - *Ich ärgere mich über den Stau.* (I'm annoyed by the traffic jam.)

9. **Mit dem Kopf durch die Wand** (With the head through the wall)
   - Meaning: To be stubborn/forceful
   - *Er will es mit dem Kopf durch die Wand.* (He wants to force it through.)

### Money & Work

10. **Da haben wir den Salat** (There we have the salad)
    - Meaning: There's the problem/mess
    - *Das war teuer, und da haben wir den Salat.* (That was expensive, and now we have a problem.)

11. **Umsonst ist der Tod** (Free is death)
    - Meaning: Nothing is truly free
    - *Glaub nicht, dass das umsonst ist.* (Don't believe that's free.)

12. **Sein Geld für etwas建成** (To build one's money for something)
    - Meaning: To invest money wisely
    - *Er hat sein Geld in dieses Haus建成.* (He invested his money in this house.)

### People & Behavior

13. **Jemandem auf den Keks gehen** (To get on someone's cookie)
    - Meaning: To annoy someone
    - *Du gehst mir langsam auf den Keks.* (You're starting to annoy me.)

14. **Den Nagel auf den Kopf treffen** (To hit the nail on the head)
    - Meaning: To be exactly right
    - *Du hast den Nagel auf den Kopf getroffen.* (You're exactly right.)

15. **Jemanden auf die Palme bringen** (To bring someone onto the palm tree)
    - Meaning: To make someone angry
    - *Hör auf, du bringst mich noch auf die Palme.* (Stop it, you're making me angry.)

### Time & Opportunity

16. **Jetzt schlägt's 13** (Now it strikes 13)
    - Meaning: Now it's really bad/extreme
    - *Das war schon schlimm, aber jetzt schlägt's 13.* (That was bad, but now it's extreme.)

17. **Da liegt der Hund begraben** (That's where the dog is buried)
    - Meaning: That's the root of the problem
    - *Da liegt der Hund begraben, das ist das Problem.* (That's the root of the problem.)

18. **Es fünf gerade sein lassen** (To let five be even)
    - Meaning: To be flexible/casual about something
    - *Lass es fünf gerade sein.* (Just let it be/be casual about it.)

### Action & Movement

19. **Da steppt der Bär** (That's where the bear dances)
    - Meaning: That's where the action is
    - *Auf der Party steppt der Bär.* (That's where the party's at.)

20. **Jemandem auf die Sprünge helfen** (To help someone with the jumps)
    - Meaning: To help someone figure something out
    - *Kannst du mir auf die Sprünge helfen?* (Can you give me a hint?)

### Success & Failure

21. **Lachen ist gesund** (Laughter is healthy)
    - Meaning: Similar to "laughter is the best medicine"
    - *Lachen ist gesund, besonders in schwierigen Zeiten.* (Laughter is healthy, especially in difficult times.)

22. **Schuster, bleib bei deinen Leisten** (Shoemaker, stay at your lasts)
    - Meaning: Stick to what you know
    - *Schuster, bleib bei deinen Leisten.* (Stick to what you're good at.)

23. **Wer nicht wagt, der nicht gewinnt** (Who doesn't dare, doesn't win)
    - Meaning: Nothing ventured, nothing gained
    - *Wer nicht wagt, der nicht gewinnt.* (You have to take risks to succeed.)

### Funny Animal Idioms

24. **Einen Vogel haben** (To have a bird)
    - Meaning: To be crazy
    - *Du hast wohl einen Vogel.* (You must be crazy.)

25. **Da haben wir den Salat** (There we have the salad) — see #10

26. **Schwein haben** (To have a pig)
    - Meaning: To be lucky
    - *Da hast du Schwein gehabt.* (You were lucky there.)

### Food-Related Idioms

27. **Tomaten auf den Augen haben** (To have tomatoes on one's eyes)
    - Meaning: To be blind to the obvious
    - *Du hast Tomaten auf den Augen!* (You're blind to the obvious!)

28. **Das ist mir Wurst** (That's sausage to me)
    - Meaning: I don't care
    - *Ob wir gehen oder bleiben, das ist mir Wurst.* (Whether we go or stay, I don't care.)

29. **Nicht alle Tassen im Schrank haben** (Not to have all cups in the cupboard)
    - Meaning: To be crazy
    - *Der hat nicht alle Tassen im Schrank.* (He's not all there.)

### Classic German Sayings

30. **Aller Anfang ist schwer** (All beginnings are difficult)
    - Meaning: Starting is always the hardest part
    - *Aller Anfang ist schwer.* (Getting started is always tough.)

## How to Use German Idioms Correctly

**Don't translate literally!** German idioms often have completely different meanings than their literal translations.

**Example:**
- ❌ *Ich habe Tomaten auf den Augen.* (Don't say this to an English speaker)
- ✅ *I'm blind to the obvious.* (This is what it means)

**Best practices:**
1. Learn the whole expression, not word-for-word
2. Practice with native speakers
3. Use context to understand meaning
4. Don't overuse them — they can sound unnatural if forced

## German Idioms vs. English Equivalents

**Some German idioms have English equivalents:**

- *Den Nagel auf den Kopf treffen* = To hit the nail on the head ✓
- *Wer nicht wagt, der nicht gewinnt* = Nothing ventured, nothing gained ✓
- *Aller Anfang ist schwer* = All beginnings are difficult ✓

**Others are uniquely German:**
- *Ich verstehe nur Bahnhof* = No English equivalent
- *Das ist nicht mein Bier* = No English equivalent
- *Da steppt der Bär* = No English equivalent

## Regional German Idioms

**Different regions have different idioms:**

- **Northern Germany:** More maritime idioms (*alle Neune sein* — to be all nines)
- **Southern Germany:** More mountain idioms (*nicht das Gelb vom Ei* — not the yellow from the egg)
- **Berlin:** Unique local idioms (*Digga* expressions)

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

**Mistake 1:** Taking idioms literally
- Don't try to understand German idioms word-for-word
- Learn the actual meaning, not the literal translation

**Mistake 2:** Using idioms in inappropriate contexts
- Some idioms are very casual
- Others can be used in formal situations

**Mistake 3:** Overusing idioms
- Don't fill every sentence with idioms
- Use them naturally and sparingly

## Practice: Real German Idiom Examples

**Example 1: At work**
*Colleague:* Warum bist du so spät?
*You:* Der Verkehr war schrecklich. Da haben wir den Salat.
*Colleague:* Ja, das verstehe ich. Ich habe Tomaten auf den Augen und den falschen Bus genommen.

Translation:
*Colleague:* Why are you so late?
*You:* The traffic was terrible. That's the problem.
*Colleague:* Yeah, I understand. I was blind to the obvious and took the wrong bus.

**Example 2: Making plans**
*Friend:* Was machen wir heute Abend?
*You:* Ich weiß nicht. Auf der Party steppt der Bär?
*Friend:* Ja, da können wir hingehen. Da steppt der Bär.

Translation:
*Friend:* What are we doing tonight?
*You:* I don't know. Is that where the action is?
*Friend:* Yeah, we can go there. That's where the party's at.

**Example 3:** Discussing a problem
*Friend:* Ich habe das Problem nicht verstanden.
*You:* Da liegt der Hund begraben. Das ist kompliziert.
*Friend:* Ja, ich muss mir den Kopf zerbrechen.

Translation:
*Friend:* I didn't understand the problem.
*You:* That's the root of the issue. It's complicated.
*Friend:* Yeah, I need to think hard about it.
