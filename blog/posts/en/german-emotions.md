# German Emotions and Feelings: Complete Guide to Gefühle Vocabulary

You're learning German. You want to express how you feel.

You freeze. **Ich fühle mich glücklich.** How do you say "sad", "angry", or "excited" in German? What about describing your emotions?

German emotion vocabulary (Gefühle) is essential for communication — from daily conversations to meaningful connections.

**The solution:** Master German feeling vocabulary with clear categories, proper articles, and cultural context.

## Basic Emotions

**Fundamental feelings:**

### Positive Emotions

1. **Glücklich** (happy)
   - **Use:** General happiness
   - **Example:** "Ich bin glücklich." (I am happy.)

2. **Fröhlich** (cheerful)
   - **Use:** Upbeat attitude
   - **Example:** "Sie ist fröhlich." (She is cheerful.)

3. **Zufrieden** (satisfied/content)
   - **Use:** Contentment
   - **Example:** "Er ist zufrieden." (He is satisfied.)

4. **Entspannt** (relaxed)
   - **Use:** Calm state
   - **Example:** "Ich bin entspannt." (I am relaxed.)

5. **Begeistert** (excited)
   - **Use:** Enthusiastic
   - **Example:** "Wir sind begeistert." (We are excited.)

6. **Erfreut** (delighted)
   - **Use:** Very pleased
   - **Example:** "Ich bin erfreut." (I am delighted.)

7. **Stolz** (proud)
   - **Use:** Pride
   - **Example:** "Er ist stolz auf dich." (He is proud of you.)

## Expressing Feelings

**Essential phrases:**

61. **Ich fühle mich...** (I feel...)
    - **Example:** "Ich fühle mich glücklich." (I feel happy.)

62. **Ich bin...** (I am...)
    - **Example:** "Ich bin müde." (I am tired.)

63. **Wie fühlst du dich?** (How do you feel?)
    - **Use:** Asking about feelings

64. **Mir geht es...** (I am...)
    - **Example:** "Mir geht es gut." (I am well.)

65. **Mir geht es nicht gut.** (I am not well.)
    - **Use:** Feeling bad

66. **Ich habe ... Gefühle.** (I have ... feelings.)
    - **Example:** "Ich habe gemischte Gefühle." (I have mixed feelings.)

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

### Mistake 1: Wrong Verb

❌ "Ich bin glücklich über" (incorrect)
✅ "Ich freue mich über" (correct)

**Rule:** Use "sich freuen über" for being happy about something.

### Mistake 2: Wrong Preposition

❌ "Ich verliebe in dich" (incorrect)
✅ "Ich bin verliebt in dich" (correct)

**Rule:** Use "verliebt in" for being in love.

### Mistake 3: Confusing Similar Emotions

❌ Confusing "wütend" (angry) with "verärgert" (annoyed)
✅ **Wütend** = stronger anger
✅ **Verärgert** = milder irritation
