# German Slang & Colloquial Expressions: Sound Like a Native Speaker

You're learning German, making great progress, and then you hear Germans talking:

*Das ist krass!* (That's awesome!)
*Geil, Mann!* (Cool, dude!)
*Alter, was machst du?* (Dude, what are you doing?)

None of this was in your textbook. German slang sounds completely different — more alive, more casual, more REAL.

German slang and colloquial expressions are what you hear in the streets, in bars, among friends. And they're easier to learn than you think.

## Why Learn German Slang?

**Textbook German = Formal. Slang = REAL.**

- *Wie heißt du?* (How are you?) — textbook, rarely used
- *Wie geht's?* (How's it going?) — what Germans ACTUALLY say

- *Das ist gut.* (That is good.) — boring
- *Das ist krass!* (That's awesome!) — what Germans ACTUALLY say

If you want to sound authentic, you need slang. Period.

## The Top 20 German Slang Words

**Memorize these 20 — they cover 80% of casual German:**

1. **Krass** (awesome, cool)
   - *Das Auto ist krass!* (That car is awesome!)
   - Can also mean "terrible" depending on context

2. **Geil** (cool, awesome)
   - *Das ist geil!* (That's cool!)
   - Originally meant "horny" but now just means "cool"

3. **Alter** (dude, man)
   - *Alter, was geht?* (Dude, what's up?)
   - Literally "old one" but used like "bro"

4. **Mega** (very, super)
   - *Das ist mega cool!* (That's super cool!)
   - Used as an intensifier

5. **Hamma** (hanging out, chilling)
   - *Lass uns hamma.* (Let's hang out.)
   - Short for *herumhängen*

6. **Keine Ahnung** (no clue, no idea)
   - *Ich habe keine Ahnung.* (I have no clue.)
   - Often shortened to *keine Ahnung* or just *keine Planung*

7. **Planung** (clue, idea)
   - *Ich habe keine Planung.* (I have no clue.)
   - Slang for "no idea"

8. **Geilomat** (show-off, braggart)
   - *Er ist so ein Geilomat.* (He's such a show-off.)
   - Someone who constantly boasts

9. **Versager** (someone who cancels)
   - *Du bist so ein Versager.* (You always cancel.)
   - From *versagen* (to cancel)

10. **Schleimer** (sycophant, kiss-ass)
    - *Er ist ein Schleimer.* (He's a kiss-ass.)
    - Someone who's overly eager to please

11. **Gönn dir** (give yourself a break)
    - *Gönn dir Zeit.* (Take your time.)
    - Short for *gönnen* (to grant/allow)

12. **Knarren** (to whine, complain)
    - *Hör auf zu knarren!* (Stop whining!)
    - From *narren* (to complain)

13. **Labern** (to learn, study)
    - *Ich muss noch labern.* (I still have to study.)
    - Casual word for *lernen*

14. **Kanake** (German of Turkish descent — can be offensive)
    - Be careful with this one
    - Can be used neutrally among friends or as an insult

15. **Kumpel** (buddy, pal)
    - *Er ist mein Kumpel.* (He's my buddy.)
    - Informal but friendly

16. **Typ** (guy, dude)
    - *Er ist ein ganz guter Typ.* (He's a really nice guy.)
    - Very common, casual

17. **Mädchen** (girl, young woman)
    - *Das Mädchen da ist hübsch.* (That girl over there is cute.)
    - Standard but informal

18. **Junge** (boy, young man)
    - *Der Junge da ist cool.* (That boy over there is cool.)
    - Standard but informal

19. **Baba** (grandma, but also "wimp")
    - *Sei nicht so eine Baba.* (Don't be such a wimp.)
    - Can be playful or insulting

20. **Oida** (dude, bro — Austrian/South German)
    - *Oida, wie geht's?* (Dude, how's it going?)
    - Very casual, regional

## Casual Greetings & Responses

**Real German greetings (not textbook versions):**

- **Na?** (Hey, what's up?) — *Na, was geht?*
- **Was geht?** (What's happening?) — *Was geht ab?*
- **Wie läuft's?** (How's it going?) — *Alles gut bei dir.*
- **Geilst du noch?** (Are you still around?) — *Ja, ich bin noch da.*

**Casual responses:**
- *Naja, so-so.* (Meh, so-so.)
- *Garnicht so gut.* (Not so good.)
- *Could be worse.* (Könnte schlimmer sein.) — *Es könnte schlimmer sein.*
- *Don't worry.* (Mach dir keine Sorgen.) — *Kein Stress.*

## Common Colloquial Phrases

**Learn these as complete phrases:**

- **Lass mal** (Come on, let's...)
  - *Lass mal gehen.* (Let's go.)

- **Mal gucken** (just looking)
  - *Ich guck nur mal.* (I'm just looking.)

- **Kein Ding** (no problem / no big deal)
  - *Das ist kein Ding.* (That's no big deal.)

- **Kein Stress** (no worries / don't stress)
  - *Mach keinen Stress.* (Don't worry about it.)

- **Ich schaff das** (I can do that)
  - *Ich schaff das heute.* (I can do that today.)

- **Das zieht** (that takes time/effort)
  - *Das zieht noch.* (That'll take a while.)

- **Gib mir bitte** (give me please — casual)
  - *Gib mir mal die Butter.* (Pass me the butter.)

- **Schnack mal** (hurry up)
  - *Schnack mal ein bisschen.* (Hurry up a bit.)

## Common Shortening Patterns

**Germans LOVE to shorten words:**

- *machen* → *mach* (do)
  - *Mach das.* (Do that.)

- *sagen* → *sag* (say)
  - *Sag mal.* (Tell me.)

- *schauen* → *guck* (look)
  - *Guck mal da.* (Look over there.)

- *oben* → *oben* (up) — no change but often shortened

- *nicht* → *nett* (not) — sometimes sounds like *net*
- *eins* → *ein* (one) — *Ich brauche ein.* (I need one.)

## Internet & Texting Slang

**Modern German slang for the digital age:**

- **Liken** (to like something)
  - *Ich like das.* (I like that.)
  - From English "like"

- **followen** (to follow someone)
  - *Ich follow dir auf Insta.* (I follow you on Instagram.)

- **posten** (to post something)
  - *Er postet das.* (He's posting that.)

- **chatten** (to chat)
  - *Wollen wir chatten?* (Do you want to chat?)

- **online sein** (to be online)
  - *Bist du noch online?* (Are you still online?)

## Filler Words & Sounds

**Germans use these constantly in casual speech:**

- **halt** (just, simply — emphasis)
  - *Das ist halt so.* (That's just how it is.)

- **mal** (just — softens commands)
  - *Guck mal.* (Just look.)
  - *Warte mal.* (Just wait.)

- **eben** (exactly, precisely)
  - *Das ist eben so.* (That's exactly how it is.)

- **schon** (already)
  - *Ich habe das schon gesehen.* (I've already seen that.)

- **ja** (yes) — *Jo* (casual)
  - *Jo, klar.* (Yeah, sure.)

- **nein** (no) — *Nö* (casual/slangy)
  - *Nö, macht kein Sinn.* (No, that makes no sense.)

## Compliment Slang

**Casual ways to compliment someone:**

- **Du bist der Hammer** (You're awesome)
  - Literally "you are the hammer"
  - *Das hast du gut gemacht!* (Well done!)

- **Du bist der Burner** (You're amazing)
  - Very common compliment

- **Respekt** (Respect)
  - *Respekt, das hast du geil hinbekommen.* (Respect, you handled that well.)

- **Full approve** (Full approval)
  - *Ich gebe dir volle Naklar.* (I give you full approval.)

## Insult Slang (Use Carefully!)

**These can be playful or insulting depending on context:**

- **Du spinnst** (You're crazy)
  - Playful: *Du spinnst ja!* (You're crazy!)
  - Serious: *Spinn nicht so.* (Don't talk nonsense.)

- **Du hast einen Vogel** (You're crazy)
  - Literally "you have a bird"
  - Common but can be offensive

- **Hau ab** (Get lost / Go away)
  - *Hau ab!* (Get lost!)
  - Can be aggressive

- **Du irrst** (You're wrong)
  - *Du irrst dich.* (You're mistaken.)
  - Polite way to disagree

## Regional Slang (Bonus)

**Different regions have different slang:**

- **Berlin:** *Digga* (dude, bro)
- **Bavaria:** *Servas* (servant) — used playfully
- **Hamburg:** *Kiez* (neighborhood) — local pride
- **Rhineland:** *Alaf* (hello) — casual greeting

**Memory hack:** Stick to universal slang first. Regional slang can confuse things.

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

**Mistake 1:** Using slang in formal situations
- Don't use *Krass* or *Alter* with your boss (unless you're close)
- Keep slang for friends and casual settings

**Mistake 2:** Overusing slang
- Don't fill every sentence with *halt*, *mal*, *echt*
- Use them naturally, not forced

**Mistake 3:** Getting the tone wrong
- *Geil* can be inappropriate in formal contexts
- *Alter* is very casual — don't use it with strangers unless you're young

## Memory Hacks That Work

**The "Krass Rule":**
- Positive context = "awesome"
- Negative context = "terrible"
- Pay attention to tone of voice

**The "Alter" Pattern:**
- *Alter* is like "bro" or "dude"
- Only use with guys you're close with
- Never use with strangers

**The "Mal" Softener:**
- Add *mal* to soften any command
- *Guck mal.* instead of *Guck!* (more casual)

## Why German Slang Is So Fun

German slang is creative, expressive, and constantly evolving. It's how Germans REALLY speak with each other.

**Best part?** Most German slang is easy to learn because it follows patterns:
- Shortening words (*mach* not *machen*)
- Adding intensifiers (*mega* before adjectives)
- Creating new words from existing ones (*Geilomat*)

And unlike textbook German, slang is ALIVE. It changes, it adapts, it reflects how Germans actually live today.

## When to Use Slang (And When NOT To)

**USE slang with:**
- Friends and family
- Casual social situations
- Texting and messaging
- People your age or younger

**DON'T use slang with:**
- Your boss (unless you're very close)
- Formal business meetings
- People significantly older than you
- Strangers (unless you're young & in a casual setting)

**When in doubt:** Start formal. If they use slang with you, you can use it back.

## The Bottom Line

German slang makes you sound AUTHENTIC, not like a textbook.

Learn the top 20 words, master the casual greetings, and German stops sounding formal and starts sounding REAL.

And if all else fails? Just say: *Alter, das ist krass!* — and fit right in.
