# German Body Parts: Complete Guide to Körperteile Vocabulary

You're learning German. You have a headache or need to describe an injury.

You freeze. **Mein Kopf tut weh.** How do you say "arm", "leg", or "stomach ache" in German? What if you need to see a doctor?

German body parts vocabulary is essential for everyday life — from describing pain to medical situations.

**The solution:** Master German body vocabulary (Körperteile) with clear categories, proper articles, and practical health phrases.

## Head and Face

**Start from the top:**

### Head

1. **Der Kopf** (the head)
   - **Use:** General head area
   - **Example:** "Mein Kopf tut weh." (My head hurts.)
   - **Common:** Kopfschmerzen (headache)

2. **Das Gesicht** (the face)
   - **Use:** Facial area
   - **Example:** "Sie hat ein schönes Gesicht." (She has a beautiful face.)

### Eyes and Vision

3. **Das Auge** (the eye) — plural: die Augen
   - **Use:** Vision organ
   - **Example:** "Meine Augen sind blau." (My eyes are blue.)
   - **Medical:** Augenarzt (eye doctor)

4. **Die Augenbraue** (the eyebrow)
   - **Use:** Facial feature
   - **Example:** "Sie hat dunkle Augenbrauen." (She has dark eyebrows.)

5. **Die Wimper** (the eyelash)
   - **Use:** Eyelash
   - **Example:** "Sie hat lange Wimpern." (She has long eyelashes.)

### Nose and Mouth

6. **Die Nase** (the nose)
   - **Use:** Smell organ
   - **Example:** "Meine Nase läuft." (My nose is running.)
   - **Common:** Schnupfen (cold)

7. **Der Mund** (the mouth)
   - **Use:** Eating and speaking
   - **Example:** "Mach den Mund auf." (Open your mouth.)
   - **Medical:** Zahnarzt (dentist)

8. **Die Lippe** (the lip)
   - **Use:** Mouth part
   - **Example:** "Sie trägt Lippenstift." (She wears lipstick.)

9. **Die Zunge** (the tongue)
   - **Use:** Taste and speech
   - **Example:** "Die Zunge schmeckt das Essen." (The tongue tastes the food.)

10. **Der Zahn** (the tooth) — plural: die Zähne
    - **Use:** Chewing
    - **Example:** "Ich habe Zahnschmerzen." (I have a toothache.)
    - **Common:** Zähneputzen (brushing teeth)

### Ears and Hearing

11. **Das Ohr** (the ear) — plural: die Ohren
    - **Use:** Hearing organ
    - **Example:** "Meine Ohren tun weh." (My ears hurt.)
    - **Medical:** Ohrenschmerzen (earache)

### Hair and Neck

12. **Das Haar** (the hair) — plural: die Haare
    - **Use:** Head hair
    - **Example:** "Sie hat lange Haare." (She has long hair.)
    - **Colors:** blond, braun, schwarz, rot

13. **Der Hals** (the neck/throat)
    - **Use:** Neck or throat area
    - **Example:** "Mein Hals tut weh." (My throat hurts.)
    - **Medical:** Halsschmerzen (sore throat)

## Upper Body

**Torso and shoulders:**

### Shoulders and Chest

14. **Die Schulter** (the shoulder)
    - **Use:** Joint area
    - **Example:** "Sie zuckt mit den Schultern." (She shrugs her shoulders.)

15. **Der Rücken** (the back)
    - **Use:** Back area
    - **Example:** "Mein Rücken tut weh." (My back hurts.)
    - **Common:** Rückenschmerzen (back pain)

16. **Die Brust** (the chest/breast)
    - **Use:** Chest area
    - **Example:** "Er hat Schmerzen in der Brust." (He has chest pain.)
    - **Medical:** Brustschmerzen (chest pain)

### Stomach Area

17. **Der Bauch** (the belly/stomach)
    - **Use:** Abdominal area
    - **Example:** "Mein Bauch tut weh." (My stomach hurts.)
    - **Common:** Bauchschmerzen (stomach ache)

18. **Der Magen** (the stomach organ)
    - **Use:** Digestive organ
    - **Example:** "Ich habe Magenschmerzen." (I have stomach pain.)

19. **Die Niere** (the kidney)
    - **Use:** Internal organ
    - **Example:** "Die Nieren sind wichtig." (The kidneys are important.)

20. **Die Leber** (the liver)
    - **Use:** Internal organ
    - **Example:** "Die Leber ist ein wichtiges Organ." (The liver is an important organ.)

## Arms and Hands

**Upper limbs:**

### Arm Parts

21. **Der Arm** (the arm)
    - **Use:** Upper limb
    - **Example:** "Heben Sie den Arm." (Lift your arm.)

22. **Das Gelenk** (the joint)
    - **Use:** Joint connection
    - **Example:** "Mein Kniegelenk tut weh." (My knee joint hurts.)

23. **Der Ellbogen** (the elbow)
    - **Use:** Arm joint
    - **Example:** "Sie stieß sich den Ellbogen." (She bumped her elbow.)

24. **Das Handgelenk** (the wrist)
    - **Use:** Wrist joint
    - **Example:** "Sie trug eine Uhr am Handgelenk." (She wore a watch on her wrist.)

### Hand Parts

25. **Die Hand** (the hand) — plural: die Hände
    - **Use:** Grasping organ
    - **Example:** "Zeig mir deine Hände." (Show me your hands.)

26. **Der Finger** (the finger) — plural: die Finger
    - **Use:** Digits
    - **Example:** "Ich habe fünf Finger an jeder Hand." (I have five fingers on each hand.)
    - **Specific:** Daumen (thumb), Zeigefinger (index finger), Mittelfinger (middle finger), Ringfinger (ring finger), kleiner Finger (pinky)

27. **Der Daumen** (the thumb)
    - **Use:** First finger
    - **Example:** "Sie zeigt mit dem Daumen nach oben." (She thumbs up.)

28. **Der Fingernagel** (the fingernail)
    - **Use:** Nail on finger
    - **Example:** "Sie lackiert ihre Fingernägel." (She paints her fingernails.)

## Lower Body

**Hips and buttocks:**

29. **Die Hüfte** (the hip)
    - **Use:** Hip joint
    - **Example:** "Sie hat Schmerzen in der Hüfte." (She has pain in her hip.)

30. **Das Gesäß** (the buttocks)
    - **Use:** Lower posterior
    - **Example:** "Sitzen Sie auf das Gesäß." (Sit on your buttocks.)

## Legs and Feet

**Lower limbs:**

### Leg Parts

31. **Das Bein** (the leg) — plural: die Beine
    - **Use:** Lower limb
    - **Example:** "Mein Bein tut weh." (My leg hurts.)

32. **Der Oberschenkel** (the thigh)
    - **Use:** Upper leg
    - **Example:** "Er trainiert seine Oberschenkel." (He trains his thighs.)

33. **Das Knie** (the knee)
    - **Use:** Knee joint
    - **Example:** "Mein Knie ist geschwollen." (My knee is swollen.)
    - **Common:** Kniescheibe (kneecap)

34. **Die Wade** (the calf)
    - **Use:** Lower leg muscle
    - **Example:** "Meine Waden tun weh." (My calves hurt.)

### Foot Parts

35. **Der Fuß** (the foot) — plural: die Füße
    - **Use:** Lower extremity
    - **Example:** "Meine Füße sind müde." (My feet are tired.)

36. **Die Ferse** (the heel)
    - **Use:** Back of foot
    - **Example:** "Sie trägt Schuhe mit hohen Absätzen." (She wears high-heeled shoes.)

37. **Der Zeh** (the toe) — plural: die Zehen
    - **Use:** Foot digits
    - **Example:** "Ich habe zehn Zehen." (I have ten toes.)

38. **Der Fußnagel** (the toenail)
    - **Use:** Nail on toe
    - **Example:** "Er schneidet seine Fußnägel." (He cuts his toenails.)

## Internal Organs

**Inside the body:**

39. **Das Herz** (the heart)
    - **Use:** Circulatory organ
    - **Example:** "Mein Herz schlägt schnell." (My heart beats fast.)
    - **Medical:** Herzinfarkt (heart attack)

40. **Die Lunge** (the lung)
    - **Use:** Breathing organ
    - **Example:** "Rauchen schadet der Lunge." (Smoking harms the lungs.)

41. **Das Gehirn** (the brain)
    - **Use:** Thinking organ
    - **Example:** "Das Gehirn steuert den Körper." (The brain controls the body.)

42. **Die Blase** (the bladder)
    - **Use:** Urinary organ
    - **Example:** "Ich muss auf die Blase." (I need to use the bathroom.)

## Skin and Other

43. **Die Haut** (the skin)
    - **Use:** Body covering
    - **Example:** "Sie hat empfindliche Haut." (She has sensitive skin.)
    - **Medical:** Hautarzt (dermatologist)

44. **Das Blut** (the blood)
    - **Use:** Circulatory fluid
    - **Example:** "Ich gebe Blut." (I donate blood.)

45. **Der Knochen** (the bone) — plural: die Knochen
    - **Use:** Skeletal structure
    - **Example:** "Er hat einen gebrochenen Knochen." (He has a broken bone.)

46. **Der Muskel** (the muscle)
    - **Use:** Movement tissue
    - **Example:** "Sport trainiert die Muskeln." (Sports train the muscles.)

## Cultural Notes

### German Health Culture

- **Health insurance:** Mandatory health insurance system
- **Pharmacy first:** Germans often visit pharmacists (Apotheker) before doctors
- **Sick leave:** Strong sick leave culture (Krankmeldung)
- **Alternative medicine:** Popular alongside conventional medicine

### Medical System

- **General practitioner:** Hausarzt (primary care doctor)
- **Specialists:** Facharzt (specialist doctor)
- **Emergency:** Notaufnahme (emergency room)
- **Emergency number:** 112 (European emergency number)

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

### Mistake 1: Wrong Article

❌ "Der Kopf" (correct) vs "Die Kopf" (incorrect)

**Rule:** "Kopf" is masculine, not feminine.

### Mistake 2: Wrong Preposition

❌ "Ich habe Schmerz an Kopf" (incorrect)
✅ "Ich habe Kopfschmerzen" (correct)

**Rule:** Use -schmerzen suffix for pain, not "Schmerz an [body part]".

### Mistake 3: Wrong Plural

❌ "Die Kopfs" (incorrect)
✅ "Die Köpfe" (correct)

**Rule:** Many body parts have irregular plurals (Kopf → Köpfe, Fuß → Füße).

## The Bottom Line

German body parts vocabulary follows clear patterns:

1. **Head and face** — Kopf, Gesicht, Auge, Nase, Mund, Ohr
2. **Upper body** — Schulter, Rücken, Brust, Bauch
3. **Arms and hands** — Arm, Hand, Finger, Ellbogen
4. **Lower body** — Hüfte, Gesäß, Bein, Fuß
5. **Internal organs** — Herz, Lunge, Magen, Gehirn
6. **Medical phrases** — Ich habe [Körperteil]schmerzen, Mein [Körperteil] tut weh

**Pro tip:** Start with **Kopf, Auge, Nase, Mund, Arm, Hand, Bein, Fuß** — these eight words cover most basic body conversations. And remember: Germans use specific pain words like "Kopfschmerzen" (headache) not "Schmerz im Kopf"!
