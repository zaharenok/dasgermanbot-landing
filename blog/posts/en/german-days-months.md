# German Days of the Week & Months: Master the German Calendar

You're learning German. Someone asks you about your schedule.

You freeze. **Welcher Tag ist heute?** What's today called in German? How do you say "Monday" or "January"? And what about abbreviations like "Mo." or "Jan."?

German calendar vocabulary is essential daily language. From scheduling appointments to understanding dates, days and months are fundamental German vocabulary you'll use constantly.

**The solution:** Master German days of the week, months, and calendar expressions with clear patterns and practical examples.

## German Days of the Week

**Start with the seven days:**

### Weekday Vocabulary

1. **Montag** (Monday)
   - **Use:** First day of work week
   - **Example:** "Am Montag arbeite ich." (I work on Monday.)
   - **Abbreviation:** Mo.

2. **Dienstag** (Tuesday)
   - **Use:** Second day
   - **Example:** "Dienstag ist mein freier Tag." (Tuesday is my day off.)
   - **Abbreviation:** Di.

3. **Mittwoch** (Wednesday)
   - **Use:** Midweek
   - **Example:** "Mittwoch haben wir ein Meeting." (We have a meeting on Wednesday.)
   - **Abbreviation:** Mi.

4. **Donnerstag** (Thursday)
   - **Use:** Fourth day
   - **Example:** "Donnerstag gehe ich zum Sport." (I go to sports on Thursday.)
   - **Abbreviation:** Do.

5. **Freitag** (Friday)
   - **Use:** Last workday
   - **Example:** "Freitag ist endlich Wochenende." (Friday is finally weekend.)
   - **Abbreviation:** Fr.

6. **Samstag** (Saturday)
   - **Use:** Weekend
   - **Example:** "Samstag schlafe ich lange." (I sleep late on Saturday.)
   - **Abbreviation:** Sa.

7. **Sonntag** (Sunday)
   - **Use:** Rest day
   - **Example:** "Sonntag ist Ruhetag." (Sunday is a rest day.)
   - **Abbreviation:** So.

## Days of the Week Patterns

**German treats weekdays differently:**

### Work Week vs Weekend

8. **Wochentage** (weekdays)
   - **Use:** Monday-Friday
   - **Example:** "An Wochentagen bin ich beschäftigt." (I'm busy on weekdays.)

9. **Wochenende** (weekend)
   - **Use:** Saturday-Sunday
   - **Example:** "Am Wochenende ruhe ich mich aus." (I rest on the weekend.)

### Day Prepositions

10. **Am Montag** (on Monday)
    - **Use:** Specific day
    - **Pattern:** am + [day name]
    - **Context:** Scheduling, routines

11. **An Wochentagen** (on weekdays)
    - **Use:** Weekdays generally
    - **Pattern:** an + plural
    - **Context:** Regular schedules

## German Months

**All twelve months in German:**

### Monthly Vocabulary

12. **Januar** (January)
    - **Use:** First month
    - **Example:** "Im Januar ist es kalt." (It's cold in January.)
    - **Abbreviation:** Jan.

13. **Februar** (February)
    - **Use:** Second month
    - **Example:** "Februar ist der kürzeste Monat." (February is the shortest month.)
    - **Abbreviation:** Feb.

14. **März** (March)
    - **Use:** Third month, spring begins
    - **Example:** "Im März beginnt der Frühling." (Spring begins in March.)
    - **Abbreviation:** Mär.

15. **April** (April)
    - **Use:** Fourth month
    - **Example:** "Im April regnet es oft." (It often rains in April.)
    - **Abbreviation:** Apr.

16. **Mai** (May)
    - **Use:** Fifth month
    - **Example:** "Mai ist wunderschön." (May is beautiful.)
    - **Abbreviation:** Mai

17. **Juni** (June)
    - **Use:** Sixth month
    - **Example:** "Im Juni ist es warm." (It's warm in June.)
    - **Abbreviation:** Jun.

18. **Juli** (July)
    - **Use:** Seventh month
    - **Example:** "Juli ist der heißeste Monat." (July is the hottest month.)
    - **Abbreviation:** Jul.

19. **August** (August)
    - **Use:** Eighth month
    - **Example:** "Im August gehen wir schwimmen." (We go swimming in August.)
    - **Abbreviation:** Aug.

20. **September** (September)
    - **Use:** Ninth month
    - **Example:** "September ist der Beginn des Herbstes." (September is the beginning of autumn.)
    - **Abbreviation:** Sep.

21. **Oktober** (October)
    - **Use:** Tenth month
    - **Example:** "Im Oktober gibt es Oktoberfest." (There's Oktoberfest in October.)
    - **Abbreviation:** Okt.

22. **November** (November)
    - **Use:** Eleventh month
    - **Example:** "November ist dunkel und kalt." (November is dark and cold.)
    - **Abbreviation:** Nov.

23. **Dezember** (December)
    - **Use:** Twelfth month
    - **Example:** "Im Dezember ist Weihnachten." (Christmas is in December.)
    - **Abbreviation:** Dez.

## Seasonal Vocabulary

**German seasons and calendar terms:**

### The Four Seasons

24. **Der Frühling** (spring)
    - **Use:** March-May
    - **Example:** "Im Frühling blühen die Blumen." (Flowers bloom in spring.)

25. **Der Sommer** (summer)
    - **Use:** June-August
    - **Example:** "Der Sommer ist heiß." (Summer is hot.)

26. **Der Herbst** (autumn/fall)
    - **Use:** September-November
    - **Example:** "Im Herbst fallen die Blätter." (Leaves fall in autumn.)

27. **Der Winter** (winter)
    - **Use:** December-February
    - **Example:** "Der Winter ist kalt." (Winter is cold.)

## Date Expressions

**How to talk about dates in German:**

### Saying Dates

28. **Heute ist der...**
    - **Pattern:** Heute ist der [number]. [Month]
    - **Example:** "Heute ist der 15. Mai." (Today is May 15th.)

29. **Welches Datum haben wir heute?**
    - **Meaning:** What date do we have today?
    - **Use:** Asking the date

30. **Der erste Januar**
    - **Meaning:** January 1st
    - **Pattern:** Ordinal numbers for dates
    - **Context:** New Year's Day

31. **Der vierundzwanzigste Dezember**
    - **Meaning:** December 24th
    - **Context:** Christmas Eve

## Calendar Conversations

**Practical date and time talk:**

### Daily Schedule Talk

32. **Welcher Tag ist heute?**
    (Which day is today?)
    - **Use:** Asking the day
    - **Response:** "Heute ist Montag." (Today is Monday.)

33. **Welchen Monat haben wir jetzt?**
    (Which month do we have now?)
    - **Use:** Asking the month
    - **Response:** "Wir haben jetzt März." (We have March now.)

34. **Wann bist du geboren?**
    (When were you born?)
    - **Use:** Asking birth date
    - **Response:** "Ich bin am 12. August geboren." (I was born on August 12th.)

## Scheduling & Planning

**Making plans with dates:**

### Appointment Language

35. **Am kommenden Montag**
    - **Meaning:** Next Monday
    - **Use:** Future scheduling
    - **Example:** "Am kommenden Montag treffe ich dich." (I'll meet you next Monday.)

36. **Letzten Dienstag**
    - **Meaning:** Last Tuesday
    - **Use:** Past reference
    - **Example:** "Letzten Dienstag war ich im Kino." (I was at the cinema last Tuesday.)

37. **Nächsten Monat**
    - **Meaning:** Next month
    - **Use:** Future planning
    - **Example:** "Nächsten Monat fahre ich in den Urlaub." (I'm going on vacation next month.)

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

### Mistake 1: Wrong Preposition

❌ "Im Montag" (incorrect)
✅ "Am Montag" (correct)

**Rule:** Use "am" for days, "im" for months/seasons.

### Mistake 2: Capitalization

❌ "am montag" (incorrect)
✅ "Am Montag" (correct)

**Rule:** Days and months are ALWAYS capitalized in German.

### Mistake 3: Date Order

❌ Following English month-day order
✅ Using German day-month order

**Rule:** German uses day.month.year (15.05.2024)

## The Bottom Line

German calendar vocabulary follows clear patterns:

1. **Days of the week** — Montag through Sonntag
2. **Months** — Januar through Dezember
3. **Seasons** — Frühling, Sommer, Herbst, Winter
4. **Prepositions** — am (days), im (months/seasons)
5. **Date format** — day.month.year (DD.MM.YYYY)

**Pro tip:** Start with **"Montag"** (Monday), **"Freitag"** (Friday), and **"Januar"** (January), **"Juli"** (July) — these are the most commonly used in scheduling. And remember: ALWAYS capitalize days, months, and seasons in German — it's a hard rule with no exceptions!
