# German Text Message Abbreviations & Chat Slang: The Ultimate Guide

You've learned German grammar, mastered vocabulary, and can hold a conversation. Then you get a German text message:

*hdgdl, lg!*

*gg, mfg, cw.*

*alsa, n8.*

Wait... what? Was that even German?

Welcome to the world of German text messaging abbreviations (Abkürzungen) and chat slang. They're EVERYWHERE in Germany — WhatsApp, SMS, Instagram, Telegram. If you want to communicate like a real German, you need to understand these digital shortcuts.

## Why Learn German Text Abbreviations?

**Textbook German ≠ Real German Digital Communication.**

Germans LOVE abbreviations (die Abkürzungen). They use them in:
- Text messages and WhatsApp
- Social media comments
- Forum posts
- Casual emails
- Gaming chats

**The reality:** If you don't know these abbreviations, you'll be lost in real German digital communication.

## The Essential German Text Abbreviations

**Master these 30 and you'll understand 90% of German text messages:**

### Greetings & Sign-offs

1. **hdgdl** (hab dich ganz doll lieb) — love you lots
   - *hdgdl, Mama* (Love you lots, Mom)
   - Super common in families and close relationships

2. **hdgl** (hab dich ganz lieb) — love you (slightly less intense)
   - *hdgl!* (Love you!)
   - Between friends and partners

3. **lg** (liebe Grüße) — best regards
   - *lg, Anna* (Best regards, Anna)
   - THE most common German sign-off

4. **mfg** (mit freundlichen Grüßen) — sincerely
   - *mfg, Max* (Sincerely, Max)
   - Semi-formal, used in emails

5. **vg** (viele Grüße) — many regards
   - *vg, das Team* (Many regards, the team)
   - Between lg and mfg in formality

6. **gru** (Grüße) — greetings
   - *gru an alle* (Greetings to everyone)
   - Casual hello/goodbye

### Common Reaction Words

7. **gg** (ganz gut) — pretty good
   - *Wie geht's? gg* (How are you? Pretty good)
   - Standard response to "how are you?"

8. **ok** — okay (same as English)
   - *Alles ok?* (Everything okay?)

9. **jo** (ja) — yeah (casual yes)
   - *Jo, klar.* (Yeah, sure.)
   - More casual than "ja"

10. **nö** (nein) — nope (casual no)
    - *Nö, kein Bock.* (Nope, no motivation.)
    - More casual than "nein"

11. **n8** (Nachtf) — good night
    - *Gute n8!* (Good night!)
    - Very common in evening texts

12. **gm** (Guten Morgen) — good morning
    - *gm!* (Good morning!)
    - Morning greeting shortcut

### Time & Planning

13. **alsa** (alles klar) — everything clear
    - *Alsa, bis morgen!* (Everything clear, see you tomorrow!)
    - Used when confirming plans

14. **bissl** (bisschen) — a little bit
    - *Warte bissl.* (Wait a little bit.)
    - Super common in spoken German too

15. **sofort** — immediately (sometimes abbreviated as *sof*)
    - *Bin sofort da.* (I'll be there immediately.)

16. **später** — later (sometimes *spät*)
    - *Ruf dich später an.* (Call you later.)

### Common Phrases

17. **cw** (ciao) — bye
    - *cw, wir sehen uns.* (Bye, see you.)
    - Casual goodbye

18. **pls** — please (from English)
    - *Hilf mir pls.* (Help me please.)
    - Used by younger Germans

19. **thx** — thanks (from English)
    - *thx für alles!* (Thanks for everything!)

20. **sry** — sorry (from English)
    - *sry, zu spät.* (Sorry, too late.)

### Emotions & Reactions

21. **lol** — laughing out loud
    - *Das ist lol.* (That's funny.)

22. **rofl** — rolling on floor laughing
    - *Rofl, echt jetzt?* (ROFL, really now?)

23. **omg** — oh my god
    - *Omg, nein!* (OMG, no!)

24. **wtf** — what the f*** (vulgar but common)
    - *Wtf machst du?* (WTF are you doing?)

### German-Specific Slang

25. **naja** — well/meh
    - *Naja, könnte schlimmer sein.* (Well, could be worse.)
    - Shows uncertainty or mild dissatisfaction

26. **bock** (Bock haben) — to have motivation/interest
    - *Hast du Bock?* (Do you want to? / Are you up for it?)
    - *Kein Bock.* (No motivation / Not in the mood.)

27. **krass** — crazy/awesome (depending on context)
    - *Das ist krass.* (That's crazy/awesome.)

28. **geil** — cool/awesome
    - *Das ist geil.* (That's cool.)

29. **alter** — dude/bro
    - *Alter, was geht?* (Dude, what's up?)

30. **digga** — bro/dude (Berlin slang)
    - *Digga, komm mal.* (Bro, come here.)

## Numbers in German Texts

**Germans abbreviate numbers differently:**

- **1** = *eins* or just *1*
- **2** = *zwei* or *zwo* (especially in phone numbers)
- **10** = *zehn*
- **100** = *hundert*

**Time abbreviations:**
- *10uhr* = 10 o'clock
- *15min* = 15 minutes
- *1std* = 1 hour

## German Emoticons & Emoji Meanings

**Germans use some unique emoji combinations:**

- 🙄 = *Augapfelroll* (eye roll) — sarcasm
- 💪 = *stark* (strong) — approval
- 👍 = *ok* — agreement
- 🤔 = *nachdenken* (thinking) — uncertainty
- 😂 = *lach* (laughing) — something's funny

## Context Matters: When to Use Abbreviations

**USE abbreviations with:**
- Friends and family
- Casual text messages
- Social media
- Gaming
- People your age or younger

**DON'T use abbreviations with:**
- Your boss (unless very casual)
- Formal emails
- Business documents
- People significantly older
- Professional settings

**Pro tip:** Start with full words. If they use abbreviations with you, match their style.

## Regional Differences

**Different regions use different abbreviations:**

- **Northern Germany:** More English loanwords (*pls, thx, lol*)
- **Southern Germany:** More traditional abbreviations (*mfg, vg*)
- **Berlin:** More slang (*digga, alter*)
- **Young people:** More English influence and emojis

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

**Mistake 1:** Using abbreviations in formal contexts
- Don't use *hdgdl* in a job application
- Don't use *lg* in formal business emails
- When in doubt, write it out

**Mistake 2:** Overusing abbreviations
- Don't fill every message with shortcuts
- Balance abbreviations with full words
- Keep it readable

**Mistake 3:** Getting the tone wrong
- *nö* is very casual — don't use it formally
- *jo* is slangy — use *ja* in formal contexts
- *cw* is very casual — use *auf Wiedersehen* formally

## Practice: Real German Text Examples

**Example 1: Making plans**
*Person A:* Hallo, hast du heute Abend Bock?
*Person B:* Jo, klar! Wann und wo?
*Person A:* 19uhr am Marktplatz?
*Person B:* Alsa, bis dann!

Translation:
*Person A:* Hello, do you want to do something tonight?
*Person B:* Yeah, sure! When and where?
*Person A:* 7 o'clock at the market square?
*Person B:* Everything clear, see you then!

**Example 2: Casual chat**
*Person A:* Wie war dein Tag?
*Person B:* gg, ganz schön stressig. Und deiner?
*Person A:* Naja, so-so. Habe bissl gearbeitet.
*Person B:* Ah, ok. Bis dann, muss los!

Translation:
*Person A:* How was your day?
*Person B:* Pretty good, quite stressful. And yours?
*Person A:* Well, so-so. Worked a bit.
*Person B:* Ah, okay. See you later, gotta go!

**Example 3: Family check-in**
*Mom:* Guten Morgen! hdgdl 💕
*You:* gm! hdgl, dir auch!
*Mom:* Alles ok bei dir?
*You:* Ja, alles klar. Bis später, lg!

Translation:
*Mom:* Good morning! Love you lots!
*You:* Good morning! Love you too!
*Mom:* Everything okay with you?
*You:* Yes, everything's good. See later, best regards!

## The Evolution of German Text Speak

**German texting has evolved:**

- **Early 2000s:** Lots of vowel removal (*Ich = ich*, *Hab = hab*)
- **2010s:** More English influence (*lol, pls, thx*)
- **2020s:** Emojis and voice messages replacing text
- **Future:** Voice-to-text changing everything

**What stays constant:** Germans love efficiency and shortcuts.

## Professional vs. Casual German Texting

**Casual (friends/family):**
- *hdgdl, lg, cw, jo, nö, bissl, bock*

**Semi-formal (colleagues you know well):**
- *vg, mfg, alsa, gg*

**Formal (business/unknown people):**
- Write everything out — no abbreviations
- Use proper greetings and sign-offs
- Full sentences with correct grammar

## German Texting Etiquette

**Do's:**
- Match the other person's formality level
- Use appropriate abbreviations for the relationship
- Respond within a reasonable time
- Use emojis to convey tone

**Don'ts:**
- Don't overuse abbreviations (hard to read)
- Don't use vulgar abbreviations with strangers
- Don't assume everyone knows every abbreviation
- Don't use abbreviations in important messages
