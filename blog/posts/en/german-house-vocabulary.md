# German House and Home Vocabulary: Complete Guide to Haus and Wohnung

You're learning German. You need to describe your home or look for an apartment.

You freeze. **Ich wohne in einer Wohnung.** How do you say "room", "kitchen", or "furniture" in German? What about renting and utilities?

German housing vocabulary is essential for everyday life — from describing where you live to navigating the rental market.

**The solution:** Master German house vocabulary with clear categories, rental phrases, and cultural context.

## Housing Types

**Different living arrangements:**

### Basic Types

1. **Das Haus** (the house)
   - **Use:** Detached house
   - **Example:** "Ich wohne in einem Haus." (I live in a house.)

2. **Die Wohnung** (the apartment)
   - **Use:** Flat/apartment
   - **Example:** "Sie sucht eine Wohnung." (She is looking for an apartment.)

3. **Das Einfamilienhaus** (single-family house)
   - **Use:** Detached house for one family
   - **Example:** "Sie wohnen in einem Einfamilienhaus." (They live in a single-family house.)

4. **Das Mehrfamilienhaus** (multi-family house)
   - **Use:** Building with multiple apartments
   - **Example:** "Das ist ein Mehrfamilienhaus." (That is a multi-family house.)

5. **Das Reihenhaus** (townhouse)
   - **Use:** Row house
   - **Example:** "Wir wohnen in einem Reihenhaus." (We live in a townhouse.)

## Cultural Notes

### German Rental Culture

- **Mietpreisbremse:** Rent control in some cities
- **Kaution:** Usually 2-3 months' rent as deposit
- **Mieterschutz:** Strong tenant protection laws
- **Nebenkosten:** Utilities often charged separately
- **Provision:** Estate agent fee (usually 2-3 months' rent)

### Floor Numbers

- **Erdgeschoss:** Ground floor (0)
- **Erster Stock:** First floor (1)
- **Zweiter Stock:** Second floor (2)

Note: German counting starts at 0 for ground floor!

## Common Mistakes to Avoid

### Mistake 1: Wrong Preposition

❌ "Ich wohne auf einer Wohnung" (incorrect)
✅ "Ich wohne in einer Wohnung" (correct)

**Rule:** Use "in" for living somewhere, not "auf".

### Mistake 2: Wrong Declension

❌ "Ich wohne in ein Haus" (incorrect)
✅ "Ich wohne in einem Haus" (correct)

**Rule:** After "in", use dative case for location.

### Mistake 3: Confusing Floors

❌ "Erster Stock" = first floor above ground (German)
✅ In English: "First floor" = ground floor

**Rule:** German Erdgeschoss = 0, Erster Stock = 1.
